Questions to Ask the Interviewer
Always prepare 4–5 questions. Asking smart questions shows you are serious and evaluating them too.
// TECHNICAL QUESTIONS (for technical interviewers)
1. "What does your current Salesforce architecture look like — is it a single org or multi-org, and what's the biggest technical challenge you're trying to solve right now?"

2. "What is the team's approach to code quality — do you have peer code reviews, a CI/CD pipeline, and what does your deployment process look like?"

3. "How does the team handle technical debt? Is there dedicated sprint capacity for refactoring and improvements?"

4. "What does the release cadence look like — how often do you deploy to production, and what's the testing process before production releases?"

5. "Are there any integrations in the roadmap that I would be involved in building? What external systems are you planning to connect to Salesforce?"
// ROLE AND CULTURE QUESTIONS (for HR or hiring manager)
6. "What does success look like for this role in the first 3 months and 6 months? How will my performance be evaluated?"

7. "Can you describe the team structure? How many developers, what's the ratio of senior to junior, and who would I report to?"

8. "What is the biggest challenge the person in this role will need to address first?"

9. "How does the company invest in developer growth — are there Salesforce certification sponsorships, training budgets, conference access?"

10. "Based on what you've heard about my experience today, do you have any concerns about my fit for this role that I can address?"
Question 10 is the most powerful question you can ask. It's bold, but it shows confidence and gives you a chance to handle objections before they knock you out of the process.
// ROLE & TEAM STRUCTURE QUESTIONS
4. "What does success look like for the person in this role in the first 30, 60, and 90 days?"

5. "Can you describe the team I would be joining — how many developers, what's the Salesforce experience level mix, and who would I be working most closely with?"

6. "How does the team handle code reviews and technical decisions? Is there a tech lead or architect I would collaborate with regularly?"

7. "What is the biggest technical challenge the team is currently facing that this role would help solve?"
Questions 4-7 show you are thinking about outcomes and team fit, not just the job description.
// GROWTH & LEARNING QUESTIONS
8. "Does the company sponsor Salesforce certifications? Which ones are most relevant to this team's work?"

9. "What does professional development look like here — is there a training budget, Trailhead time, conference attendance?"

10. "Where have people in this role typically gone within the company — what does the career path look like for a senior developer who performs well?"

11. "What new Salesforce features or platform capabilities is the team most excited about exploring in the next year?"
These questions signal you are serious about growing and staying long-term — what every hiring manager wants to hear.
// PROCESS & CULTURE QUESTIONS
12. "What does the deployment process look like — do you use CI/CD pipelines, and what does your change management process look like for production releases?"

13. "How does the team approach technical debt — is there sprint capacity dedicated to refactoring and improvement, or is everything feature-driven?"

14. "What collaboration tools does the team use — JIRA, Confluence, Git, Copado? What does a typical sprint look like?"

15. "How are priorities set when there are competing demands between different business stakeholders?"
Questions about process reveal whether this is a healthy engineering culture or a firefighting culture.
// CLOSING POWER QUESTIONS
16. "Based on everything we've discussed today, do you have any concerns about my fit for this role that I can address directly?"

17. "What do you personally find most rewarding about working here?"

18. "What are the next steps in the process and what is your expected timeline?"

19. "Is there anything about my background or experience that you would like me to elaborate on before we close?"

20. "If the person who previously held this role were here, what would you tell them was the most important thing to focus on?"
Question 16 is the most powerful closing question. It's bold but shows confidence and gives you a chance to address any hesitation before you leave the room.
